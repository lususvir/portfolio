Divvy Money Manager

Mobile Design

I've always found financial matters frustrating, whether it's navigating my bank's overly complex interface, or understanding deeper financial topics. In particular, I found the seemingly simple task of saving money unnecessarily difficult. I was tired of losing track of my savings goals, so I decided to develop an app.

Divvy (originally called Fundit) was an entrepreneurial effort to help people keep their funds divided into easily digestible and trackable goals. It was basically a digital version of the envelope system. The idea was to allow you to divvy up the funds in an account for different purposes. Essentially a way to save for multiple things, without multiple savings accounts.

I conducted user-research by talking to people and sending surveys. I wanted to learn how people managed their money. I found that people were doing things that suggested Divvy would be useful (e.g. opening multiple bank accounts to keep funds divided).

My initial effort was an Android app that I hacked together myself. It was a prototype that parsed balance info from my bank via SMS. I could then create envelopes (what I now call goals) and assign money to them. The app could also parse transactions and allow me to drag and drop transactions to envelopes (example: apply a debit to a specific envelope).

I used this prototype myself for a few months. Eventually I found a developer and began wire framing and prototyping a more refined solution. The final app was released in beta on Android, but due to lack of time I walked away from the project.
