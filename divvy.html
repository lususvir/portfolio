Divvy Money Manager

Mobile Design

I've always found financial matters frustrating, whether it's navigating my bank's overly complex interface, or understanding deeper financial topics (such as stocks or crypto). In particular, I found the seemingly simple task of saving money unnecessarily difficult. I was tired of losing track of my spending goals (e.g. keeping fun money separate from spending money). I didn't want to open multiple bank accounts to track goals, so I decided to develop an app.

Divvy (originally called Fundit) was an entrepreneurial effort to help people keep their funds divided into easily digestible and trackable goals. It was basically a digital version of the envelope system. The idea was to allow you to divvy up the funds in an account for different purposes. Essentially a way to save for multiple things, without multiple savings accounts.

My initial effort was an Android app that I hacked together myself. It was never intended to go to the app store. The app sent a balance command to my bank via SMS. It then parsed the incoming balance and displayed it in the app. You could then create envelopes (what I now call goals) and assign money to them. The app could also parse transactions and allow you to drag and drop transactions to envelopes (example: apply a debit to a specific envelope).

I used this prototype myself for a few months before before it broke due to bugs. And busy with other projects, I no longer had time to work on it.

Storyboarding the concept for Fundit based on survey/interview feedback.
